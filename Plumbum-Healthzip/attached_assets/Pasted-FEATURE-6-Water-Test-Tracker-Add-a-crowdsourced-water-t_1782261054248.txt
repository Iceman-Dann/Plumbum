FEATURE 6 — Water Test Tracker
Add a crowdsourced water testing layer to Plumbum where users can log real water test results, creating a verified ground-truth dataset that improves the model and is itself publishable research data.
Backend: add a test_results table to the SQLite database with fields: anonymous session ID, census tract FIPS, test date, lead concentration in ppb, test kit brand, result category (ACTION REQUIRED above 15ppb / ELEVATED 5-15ppb / SAFE below 5ppb), and an optional notes field. Add endpoints: POST /api/test-results to submit a result, GET /api/test-results?fips=... to get aggregated results for a census tract, GET /api/test-results/download to download the full anonymized dataset as CSV.
On the result page add a new section below the comparison bars with 11px all-caps gray label "CONTRIBUTE TO RESEARCH" and headline "Did you get your water tested?" Body text: "Real water test results from your community improve Plumbum's accuracy and become part of a published research dataset. Your submission is completely anonymous."
Show a simple form — no email, no name, nothing identifying: Lead concentration field (number input, ppb), test kit brand (text input, optional), test date (date picker), a checkbox "I confirm this result is from a certified test kit." Submit button in brick red labeled "Submit anonymous result."
After submission show a confirmation: "Thank you. Your result has been added to the community dataset for census tract [FIPS]. This data is publicly available for research."
On the result page below the census tract dossier add a new "Community test results" panel if test data exists for that tract: show the number of tests submitted, the average lead concentration, a small bar showing the distribution of results across the three categories, and the date of the most recent submission. If no results exist yet show "No community test results yet for this tract. Be the first →"
Add a /data page to the site — linked in the footer — showing the full aggregated dataset stats: total tests submitted, tracts covered, average lead concentration nationally, percentage of submitted tests above the EPA action level of 15ppb. A download button for the full CSV. This page is what you cite in your arXiv paper as your original crowdsourced dataset. That dataset is worth more than the model.
